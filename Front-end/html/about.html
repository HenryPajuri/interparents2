About INTERPARENTS

The official federation representing parents across all 13 European Schools, actively participating in educational decision-making and sharing best practices.

Who We Are

INTERPARENTS is the federation of all 13 European Schools Parents' Associations. We serve as the officially recognised representative of parents of all pupils in the European Schools at the Board of Governors and its subsidiary committees.

We actively participate, representing the voice of families, in the decision making of the European Schools, and share information about best practices across different European Schools Parents Associations.

Our Purpose & Role

The main purpose of INTERPARENTS is to share information about best practices across different European Schools Parents Associations and to represent the voice of families in the various governance bodies of the European Schools.

We actively participate in the decision making of the European Schools through:

  • Board of Governors meetings - The highest decision-making body
  • Joint Teaching Committee - Curriculum and educational standards
  • Budgetary Committee - Financial planning and resource allocation
  • Preparatory working groups - Policy development and implementation

Learn more about these governance bodies at www.eursc.eu/en/Office/bodies.

How We Work

According to the INTERPARENTS statutes, 4 parent representatives from each Parents' Association are invited to participate in our work. These representatives come together for INTERPARENTS meetings four times a year, organized on a rotating basis by Parent Associations in different European Schools.

Get Involved

If you are willing to travel up to 4 times a year and attend INTERPARENTS meetings held in different European Schools, as well as meetings at the Office of the Secretary General of the European Schools (OSGES) level, ask your local Parents Association how to join the local INTERPARENTS working group.

BAC Working Group Support

The INTERPARENTS BAC working group closely follows the annual BAC exams and its representatives are available to answer questions during and after final exams.

Our Commitment

INTERPARENTS remains dedicated to ensuring that the voices of parents are heard in all educational policy decisions affecting the European Schools system. Through our active participation in governance bodies, our comprehensive working groups, and our commitment to sharing best practices, we strive to maintain and improve the high standards of education across all European Schools.
